Sentence Structure

1) First Sentence Type (DAS)
a) Actor is a "Subject"

form: Describer + "ang" + Subject

  • Malípay ang batà
    The Child is happy

    • ("Malípay is the describer, meaning "happy")
b) Actor is the name of a person

form: Describer + "si" + Name

  • Malípay si Kristel
    Kristel is happy

  • Gutóm si Javier
    Javier is hungry

c) Actor is a "Subjective Pronoun"
   (I, You, He/She, we, they)

form: Describer + Pronoun

  • 25 isá
    she is 25 (years old)

  • gutóm ako
    I am hungry

2) Secont Sentence Type (VAOL)

form: Verb + Actor + Object + Location
Many sentences may not have object or location.

  • Ginkáon (verb) ko (actor) ang curry (object) sa baláy (location)
    I ate the curry in the house

  • Nagkádto (verb) si Kristelita (actor)sa market (object)
    Kristelita went to the market

  • Ginkáon (verb) sang idô(actor) ang curry (object)
    The dog ate the curry

Negation

1) In First Sentence Type (DAS)
a) Actor is a "Subject" or "Name of a person"

just add indi at the start of the sentence.

  • Indì malípay ang batà
    The Child is not happy
  • Indì malípay si Kristelita
    Kristelita is not happy
b) Actor is a "Subjective Pronoun"

In (almost) all cases pronouns must be the second word of a sentence.

  • Indì akó gutóm
    I am not hungry
2) In Second Sentence Type (VAOL)
a)Actor is Pronoun and the verb is in "past" or "present" tense

we can use either waáy or walâ. Pronouns must be the second word of a sentence.

  • Waáy/Walâ nagulán
    It didn't rain
  • Waáy/Walâ nagaulán
    It is not raining
  • Waáy/Walâ siá nagkádto sa market
    He did not go to the market
  • Waáy/Walâ niá ginakáon ang curry
    She is not eating the curry
b) Actor is Pronoun and the verb is in "future" tense or "infinitive"(no tense at all)

we must use indì. Pronouns must be the second word of a sentence.

  • Indì magulán
    It will not rain
  • Indì silá magdalá sang pagkáon
    They will not bring food
c) Actor is Subject or Name of a person

Just construct a sentence and add the appropriate negation word at the beggining.

    Past Tense
  • Walâ/Waáy nágkadto si Noynoy sa market
    Noynoy did not go to the market
  • Walâ/Waáy nagkáon si Marcus
    Marcus did not eat

    • Present Tense
  • Walâ/Waáy nagakánta si Denden
    Denden is not singing
  • Walâ/Waáy ginakáon sang idô ang curry
    The dog is not eating the curry

    • Future Tense
  • Indì magdalá ang laláke sang pagkáon
    The man will not bring food
